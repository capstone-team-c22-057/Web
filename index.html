Plant Disease

Powdery mildew

Powdery stains that leave a dusty white coating on leaves, stems, and flowers are caused by a fungus. This disease is usually experienced by a number of plants such as lilacs, apples, grapes, cucumbers, peas, phlox, daisies, and roses.

Hairy Mushroom

Downy mildew is caused by fungus-like organisms and affects many crops such as grapes, lettuce, broccoli and cauliflower. This fungus often occurs during wet weather by causing the upper parts of the leaves to change color, while the undersides of the fungus develop white and gray tones.

Black Spot

Black spot is a fungal disease that is commonly found on roses, as well as other fruit. While they don't kill the plant outright, these black spots can be debilitating and make them susceptible to other diseases. Black spot will thrive in cool, humid weather.

Mosaic Viruses

The mosaic virus usually attacks tomatoes, peppers, potatoes, apples, pears, cherries, and tobacco. Plants that are attacked by this virus, the leaves are yellow mottled which sometimes curl. Some plants show yellowing, stunted growth, deformed fruits.
